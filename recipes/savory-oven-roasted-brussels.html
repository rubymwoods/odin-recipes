Savory and Spicy Oven Roasted Brussels Sprouts

by A Family Feast

Brussels sprouts about to go in the oven and become roasty, toasty, spicy and savory.

These Brussels Sprouts are my go-to recipe for Thanksgiving. When I have the time (and the oven space), I let them roast until the edges of the leaves are crispy.

If you're in a hurry or the oven space is scarce, these work perfectly in the air fryer as well

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds Brussels sprouts
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • kosher salt and black pepper
  • garlic powder
  • paprika
  • chili flakes
  • OPTIONAL bacon crumbles

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Clean and trim Brussels sprouts and cutting any very large heads in half through the core. (It's fine if some of the outer leaves fall off - just bake those along with the rest of the sprouts. They get extra crispy and are delicious!
  3. Place the sprouts in a large bowl and drizzle with olive oil, tossing to evenly coat. Add in your favorite spices to taste.
  4. Pour the Brussels sprouts onto a large sheet pan (you want them to be in a single layer).
  5. Roast in the oven for 20 to 30 minutes, turning halfwayh through the cooking time, until golden and lightly caramelized.
  6. Serve hot.
