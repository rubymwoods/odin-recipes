Sheet Pan Chicken Enchiladas

by @karissastevens

A screenshot from Karissa Stevens' tiktok, Making A Dinner With Rotisserie Chicken, featuring family-favorite sheetpan chicken enchiladas quesadillas.

This recipe is perfect if you have leftover chicken or a rotisserie. It's quick and hands off once you add the filling.


Just put in the oven and put your feet up, 25 minutes later and you've got 6 servings. Kids and adults both love it, and it's easy to adjust to your preferences.


Ingredients

  • leftover rotisserie chicken
  • can of enchilada sauce (or make at home)
  • 1 block of cream cheese, softened
  • can of black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 8 burrito-sized flour tortillas
  • 4 oz. of cheddar cheese (half a block)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit. Shred the chicken, and mix with enchilada sauce, cream cheese, and black beans.
  2. Spray a half sheet sized sheetpan with cooking spray then layer 6 tortillas on it. Cover the bottom, with some overhang.
  3. Grate half the cheddar (2 oz) over the tortillas, then spoon the chicken mixture over.
  4. Place another 2 tortillas on top, then fold the sides in to create a closed envelope. Spray with more cooking spray.
  5. Place sheetpan on top to weigh it down and bake for 25 minutes.
